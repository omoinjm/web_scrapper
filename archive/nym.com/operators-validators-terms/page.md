## I. DEFINITIONS

Applications refers to any Software that uses, runs on or otherwise interoperates with the NYM Network whether developed by Nym Technologies SA or an independent third party.

Gateways are the nodes that serve as the entrance to the Nym Mixnet and exit from the Nym mixnet and possibly directly allow users to enter and exit from Gateways, including without mixing. They may check bandwidth credentials before sending traffic on to three layers of mix nodes. Gateways will be rewarded through a share of user fees, and optionally the mixmining pool.

Mixmining Pool Mixmining Pool is the incentivized process within the Nym Mixnet, where Mix Nodes are rewarded for their role in mixing packets, effectively preserving user privacy.

Mix Nodes means the nodes on the Nym Mixnet that mix encrypted data packets and add micro timing delays making internet traffic untraceable. Mix nodes are rewarded based on their work of providing privacy by mixing packets and their reputation (their total bonded and delegated stake).

Network Requester means the nodes on the Nym Mixnet that allow Nym users to send outbound requests from their local machine through the Nym Mixnet to a server, which then makes the request on their behalf, shielding them (and their metadata) from clearnet, untrusted and unknown infrastructure, such as email or message client servers.

Node Operators means individual or entity that runs and operates Nym Node(s) as specified in these Terms.

Nym means Nym Technologies SA, place Numa-Droz 2, 2000 Neuchâtel, Switzerland.

Nym Mixnet means the multi-purpose mixnet that is designed to prevent traffic analysis by an adversary capable of watching the entire network, including the NSA.

Nym Node refers to a network endpoint or computing device within Nym's infrastructure. Nodes may encompass servers, routers, or any other designated computing entities forming part of the network. It designates specifically four node types: i) Mix Nodes, ii) Gateways, iii) Network Requesters, iv) Validators.

NYM Token means the identically named tokens, issued by Nym Technologies SA, designed to ensure fees are paid and rewarded proportionally to the amount of stake and work done mixing and signing.

Nym Network means a network of Nym Nodes that are used to run applications and provide the encryption and sending of traffic through the networks, including usage of the Nyx Blockchain, usage of the Nym Mixnet, and the usage of the network for VPN services without the mixing of traffic, as well as any other purpose.

NymVPN means the Nym VPN Application developed by Nym Technologies SA, which provides virtual private network services that incorporate blockchain technology to enhance online privacy and security by encrypting internet traffic and leveraging blockchain for secure authentication and record-keeping.

Nyx Blockchain means the Inter-Blockchain Communication Protocol and CosmWasm-enabled Cosmos blockchain that allows the usage of zk-nym anonymous credentials. The Nyx Blockchain supports the Nym network, hosts both the NYM and NYX cryptocurrencies, the CosmWasm smart contracts keeping track of the network, and facilitating zk-nym credential generation.

Software refers to any computer program, code, scripts, algorithms, and related materials, whether in object code, source code, or any other form, that are utilized in the operation, functioning, or management of the Nym Mixnet whether developed by Nym Technologies SA or an independent third party.

Validators means the whitelisted nodes that secure the Nyx blockchain via Proof of Stake consensus. The Nyx Blockchain records the ledger of NYM transactions and executes the smart contracts for distributing NYM rewards; the validators are run via the nyxd binary (codebase), maintaining a CosmWasm- and IBC-enabled blockchain.

VPN Services means the NymVPN's virtual private network services that incorporate blockchain technology to enhance online privacy and security by encrypting internet traffic, providing anonymity, and leveraging blockchain for secure authentication and record-keeping.
